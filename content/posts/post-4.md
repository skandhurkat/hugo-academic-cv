If there's one thing I’ve learned from my career as a high-speed pursuit engineer, it’s that **overconfidence** is the enemy of success—especially when it comes to rocket-powered devices.

Take the **rocket-powered backpack**, for instance. The concept seemed flawless: a portable jetpack for high-speed mobility that could allow me to catch up with the Road Runner in mere moments. However, the moment I began testing it, I realized I had vastly underestimated the complexities of balancing fuel consumption, flight stability, and heat generation.

I had built a device with enough thrust to launch me into the air, but no way to regulate that thrust during descent. A few test runs ended in dramatic crashes—one of which involved a particularly memorable collision with a cliffside. As the rocket fueled me to greater heights, I began to realize that I had failed to account for altitude loss and wind resistance. 

Despite these setbacks, the lessons I learned with the rocket-powered backpack were invaluable. Today, I take a more measured approach to new projects, focusing on safety and contingency plans before testing any device. The Road Runner may be elusive, but I’m slowly getting better at surviving the chase.
